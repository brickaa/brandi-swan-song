It’s Been Real, Texas,
But Brandi’s Gotta Run

After emptying all of Texas’ prisons, Grissom will try her hand in California

By Acclaimed Author Michael Morton

SHARELINES

  • With Texas lockups empty, @brandigrissom heads to @LATimes to dismantle California prison system

  • .@brandigrissom plans to institute mandatory @LATimes spin classes and organic peanut butter & banana lunches

Following a successful effort to free each of Texas’ wrongfully convicted inmates — and maybe one or two rightfully convicted ones — Texas Tribune Managing Editor Brandi Grissom is taking her talents and mountain bikes west to California.

Grissom said that crime in Texas has become ho-hum.

“It’s not enough for me anymore to just write about insane criminals who jab out their eyeballs and eat them. I need crimes that are really fucked up.”

— Brandi Grissom

“It’s not enough for me anymore to just write about insane criminals who jab out their eyeballs and eat them,” Grissom said, speaking between gulps from her 5-gallon bottle of SmartWater. “I need crimes that are really fucked up.”

Grissom is taking the reins as the Los Angeles Times’ enterprise editor, with a mission to get the award-winning paper’s investigative projects and reporters in ship-shape.

Grissom secured the job by pledging to institute mandatory 6 a.m. spin classes and require newsroom lunches to include only organic peanut butter spread on bananas.

“I’m gonna trim fat wherever I see it,” Grissom said. “Word counts, waistlines, you name it.”

Grissom leaves behind a storied reporting career in Texas:

  • Her for-the-ages friendship with former state Rep. Norma “you are not my friend” Chavez
  • Her admiration for the Perry administration’s ever-so-useful (and cost effective) border cameras
  • Her confusing-to-all-of-us obsession with known murderer Bernie Tiede (oh wait, that was just a favor for Richard Linklater)
  • Her hard-hitting takedownwardfacingdog of prisons that limit yoga behind bars

Officemates say they will particularly miss the moments when Grissom, seeking desperately to shed her ties to podunk Nebraska and Kansas, pronounced the names of Hispanics with the accent and inflection of a true native — putting even Alex Trebek to shame.

Grissom says her best years were spent as an editor — harassing her overworked colleagues for stories, establishing an authoritarian projects scheduling regime and taking her fellow editors on forced sprints around Ladybird Lake, after which she’s the only one who didn’t vomit. (And she ONLY taught 7 spin classes that morning.)

“The passive-aggressive subject lines on her emails requesting story pitches will be such a challenge to emulate,” incoming Managing Editor Ayan Mittra said. “Brandi sets such a high bar.”

Grissom added that the timing couldn't be better for her move to California. Just last month, a federal district judge ruled that state's death penalty was unconstitutional. She said her first priority would be to assemble a group of intrepid journalists to help reinstate it, because, what will they have to write about otherwise?

On her own time, Grissom plans to relax with weekly swims across the Pacific Ocean and leisurely walks with Travis, Maya and Ginny from L.A. to San Francisco.

Asked what she’ll miss least about Texas and the Tribune, Grissom pondered the triple-digit summers, the late-night edits and the romantic overtures from crazy Texas inmates.

Then it dawned on her.

“The traffic here is fucking ridiculous,” Grissom said. “I’m so grateful to be going to a place where it won't be at a standstill.”
